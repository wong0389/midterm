The Siberian Forest Cat Breed

The Siberian cat is an ancient and majestic breed that originated in Russia. As forest cats, they have luxurious triple coats for cold protection. Siberians are outgoing and affectionate, making them excellent companions.

Facts About The Siberian Forest Cat

  • A thick, three-layered coat protects them from cold weather. The coat gets shorter and less dense in the summer.
  • They have a powerfully built, barrel-shaped body with hind legs that are slightly longer than their front legs, which aids their impressive jumping ability.
  • Adults typically weigh between 15 to 20 pounds, but they are considered large cats and develop slowly, reaching full maturity at about five years of age.
  • They have a round head, large, round eyes, and a friendly expression. Male Siberians often have a prominent neck ruff.
